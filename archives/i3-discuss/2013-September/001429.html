Hi Jay,

Jay Terk <areray2 at gmail.com> writes:
> Hey you guys, I just discovered i3lock.  Very nice program, but
> shouldn't it have a timeout, either built-in or user-specified?        
> The way it is now, if i3lock is running and text is entered, it 
> goes from "DPMS off" to the black screen (or whatever the user 
> configured), and it just waits there for the Enter key to be
> pressed-- and if the Enter key is never pressed, it just stays on
> the black screen.  Wouldn't a timeout be better, so that after 
> <some number> of seconds, it will timeout and revert to "DPMS off"?
The only thing i3lock does is turning off the screen when there is no
input in the buffer. Turning the screen on (and off after a timeout) is
the X11 server’s doing, so you need to check your DPMS settings.

The next time, please write to either the mailing list i3-discuss
(cc'ed) or open a bugreport in order to get a more timely response and
publicly archive the question/response.
