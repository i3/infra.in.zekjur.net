> I’d argue that you should disable swap entirely, so that the graphics
> application in question can detect an out of memory situation and
> react appropriately (by telling you about it, probably). If the app
> doesn’t work that way, maybe you can convince upstream to implement

simple answer: can't disable swap.

the situation is working with images where a single layer is anything 
from 200MB (8bit RGB) to 1GB. during an edit you can have 5 or 10 or 50 
of those, depending on your organisational skills. but at one point 
sooner or later you *will* need that swap. keeping an eye on your 
memoryusage will make your predictions better. and since memoryusage is 
*directly* related to what you do, there is no nervous checking. it's 
like a car where you make quick glances at the speedometer, mirror etc 
to check that everything is happening as expected, e.g. after shifting gear.
