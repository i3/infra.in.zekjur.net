On Mon, Jan 21, 2013 at 03:20:12AM -0800, Tai-Lin Chu wrote:
> at this moment i3wm has 4 layouts: 1. splitv 2. splith 3. tabbed 4. stacked.
>
> But I think we should remove 1 and 2 because this default setting
>  is useless. Our screen has limited width or height, so splitting more
> than 2 times will make nothing readable.

There are usecases where more than 2 splits are fine and useable.
On my big 1920x1080 monitor I can comfortably use 3 or 4 columns
depending on what I'm doing.

And it looks like you're assuming that the splits are always evenly
sized which is not the case. Think for example about a small column
which contains pidgin's buddy list. Then on most displays there's still
enough place for two other columns.

So I'm against removing splitv and splith. But I don't have anything
against adding your suggestion as an _additional_ layout.

BTW. didn't i3 have at one point the behaviour that it would toggle
between splitv and splith depending on which side was bigger? Or am I
confusing this with another window manager?
